What's real: each cell is a 9-state variable; your clues are clamped; the blanks are relaxed by many parallel Gibbs chains that resample each cell against its row, column, and 3×3 box, annealing from hot to cold. Energy = the number of conflicting pairs; when it reaches zero, the puzzle is solved. The parallel replicas are what escape local traps — the same “replicas restore ergodicity” effect measured on real THRML (EXP-009), and exactly what a thermodynamic sampling chip (TSU) provides physically instead of one-at-a-time on a CPU. Honest limit: deliberately fiendish puzzles can trap stochastic search in a near-solution for a while — hit Reheat or add replicas. Puzzle generation uses an ordinary shuffle-a-valid-grid method; the solving is all runtime. Part of the persistent stochastic runtime.
